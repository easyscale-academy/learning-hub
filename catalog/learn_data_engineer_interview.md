## Overview: Why You Need This Question Bank

### The Problem: Why Traditional Interview Prep Fails

**Problem 1: Questions are scattered, no system**
- Interview prep resources are fragmented across the web
- Quality varies wildly — it's easy to waste time on low-quality material
- No clear progression from basics to advanced, so you don't know where to start

**Problem 2: You learn it, then forget it**
- You memorize answers but don't understand *why*
- Do a question once, forget it a week later
- When the interviewer asks a variation, you're lost

**Problem 3: No feedback, false confidence**
- You do questions yourself with no way to grade them properly
- You don't know which conceptual gaps are critical
- Your weak areas stay hidden until the interview

1. **182 systematically organized questions** — Structured by level (Junior/Mid/Senior) and 45+ topics
2. **AI-guided study sessions** — The AI tool automatically selects questions, grades your answers, and pinpoints weak areas
3. **Two learning modes**
   - **Interview Mode**: Simulates real interview pressure with timed questions and immediate grading
   - **Learning Mode**: Deep explanations with the ability to ask follow-up questions

## Core Learning Philosophy

### What This Question Bank Is Really For

> This isn't about memorizing answers to trick interview questions. It's about building the mental models that help you design and operate better data systems.

**Three Core Principles:**

1. **Fundamentals over Trends**
   - Knowing *why* data needs to be partitioned is more valuable than memorizing Spark parameters
   - Understand CAP theorem, and you can solve *any* distributed systems problem

2. **Trade-off Thinking over Optimization Obsession**
   - Production systems have no "perfect" solutions, only choices with different costs
   - Latency vs. storage vs. complexity vs. operational overhead — successful engineers navigate these trade-offs

3. **Building over Reading**
   - This question bank is a starting point, not the destination
   - Real learning happens when you apply these concepts to actual data problems

### Recommended Learning Path

**Week 1: Baseline Assessment**
- Take random questions (mix of Junior/Mid/Senior)
- Identify your strong and weak areas

**Weeks 2-4: Deep Learning**
- Focus on weak topics in Learning Mode
- Build understanding of the underlying principles
- Make sure you can explain "why," not just "what"

**Week 5: Reinforce**
- Retake the same topics you struggled with
- Measure how much your understanding has improved
- Aim for consistent performance

**Ongoing: Apply**
- Use these concepts in real pipeline design and optimization
- Validate your understanding through practical work
- Come back to questions you found hard, see if you approach them differently now
