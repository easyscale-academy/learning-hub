# Financial Engineer Interview Prep

## Overview

### The Problem with Traditional Interview Prep

Preparing for Financial Engineering (Quant) interviews is painful:

- **Massive knowledge scope**: Derivatives pricing, risk modeling, stochastic calculus, system design—23 topic areas, none optional
- **Hidden knowledge gaps**: You don't know what you don't know until an interviewer exposes it
- **Inefficient studying**: Reading books and watching videos without targeted feedback
- **No real practice**: Passive learning doesn't prepare you for live Q&A

### Our Solution

An AI-powered interview question bank covering the full quantitative finance knowledge stack. Through interactive practice, you can **actively discover knowledge gaps**, **dive deep on demand**, and **track your progress** over time.

### Value Comparison

| Dimension | Traditional Approach | AI-Assisted Practice |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Finding gaps | Passive—discover in interviews | Active—random questions expose weaknesses |
| Feedback | Delayed—check answers later | Instant—detailed explanations per question |
| Deep learning | One-way—books can't answer follow-ups | Interactive—ask until you understand |
| Coverage | Hard to verify all topics covered | Systematic—track by topic and difficulty |
| Time efficiency | Low—find resources, organize notes | High—AI locates questions and explanations |

## Module 1: Interactive Practice System

### The Problem

The hardest part of self-study isn't finding materials—it's not knowing what you don't know.

You might:
- Finish a derivatives pricing textbook feeling confident
- Get asked about Girsanov's theorem's financial interpretation—and blank out
- Review VaR calculations but miss its real-world limitations

### The Solution

Two practice modes:

**Interview Mode**
- 3 multiple choice + 3 short answer questions
- Simulates real interview pacing
- Provides score summary and areas needing review
- Best for: testing current level, finding gaps

**Learning Mode**
- Deep dive on a single question
- Unlimited follow-up questions, learn at your pace
- AI explains "what it is", "why it matters", and "how to apply it"
- Best for: tackling difficult concepts, building intuition

### Personalization

- **Smart question selection**: Random, by topic, or specific question ID
- **Instant feedback**: Not just right/wrong, but why you got it wrong
- **Level assessment**: Share your resume for personalized study recommendations

### Sample Output

```
📊 Session Summary

Score: 4/6 (67%)

✅ Correct:
- E-01-01: Forward vs Futures differences
- E-02-03: Duration definition
- M-04-01: Delta Hedging mechanics
- M-07-02: Ito's Lemma application

❌ Needs Review:
- H-01-03: Exotic option pricing (switch to learning mode for deep dive)
- M-20-01: VaR limitations (see topic 20 tutorials)

💡 Suggestion: Risk modeling needs work—start with Easy questions in topic 20
```

## Module 2: Structured Question Bank

### The Problem

Interview prep resources are either scattered (forum posts, blog articles) or too academic (textbook exercises). Hard to find a systematic question bank **designed specifically for financial engineering interviews**.

### The Solution

264 questions organized by difficulty and topic:

**Three Difficulty Levels**

| Level | Focus | Sample Question |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| Easy | Core concepts—"what is it" | What's the difference between Forward and Futures? |
| Medium | Implementation—"how to build it" | How do you derive the Black-Scholes PDE? |
| Hard | Architecture—"why" and "tradeoffs" | Design a distributed risk calculation platform |

**23 Topic Areas**

Covering the financial engineering core:

- Finance Theory: Derivatives, Fixed Income, Market Microstructure, Structured Products, Regulatory Frameworks
- Math Foundations: Probability & Statistics, Stochastic Calculus, Numerical Methods, Optimization, Time Series
- Technical Implementation: Python, C++, System Design, Low-Latency Computing, Data Engineering, Cloud Computing
- Quant Practice: Machine Learning, Alternative Data, Portfolio Construction, Risk Modeling, Backtesting, Model Validation, Cross-Domain Troubleshooting

### Personalization

Choose your starting point based on experience:

| Your Background | Recommended Start |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| New to quant finance | Start with Easy, build foundations |
| 1-2 years experience | Quick Easy pass, focus on Medium |
| 3+ years experience | Dive into Medium and Hard |
| Interview prep | Match difficulty to target role level |

## Module 3: Progress Tracking

### The Problem

The biggest learning trap: feeling productive while staying in your comfort zone. The topics you avoid are exactly the ones you'll face in interviews.

### The Solution

Systematic learning loop:

```
Find Gaps → Targeted Study → Spaced Review → Verify Improvement
```

- **Find gaps**: Random questions expose unknown unknowns
- **Targeted study**: Learning mode for deep understanding
- **Spaced review**: Retest after a few days to leverage the forgetting curve
- **Verify improvement**: Compare scores to confirm actual learning

### Sample Output

```
📈 Progress Tracking

Topic 07 - Stochastic Calculus
  First test: 2/5 (40%)
  Learning mode: 3 concepts covered
  Review test: 4/5 (80%) ✅ Significant improvement

Topic 20 - Risk Modeling
  First test: 1/5 (20%)
  Status: Learning mode recommended
```

## Why This Works

### Traditional Pain Points

1. **Passive learning**: Reading without testing
2. **Selective practice**: Avoiding hard topics
3. **No feedback loop**: Right/wrong without explanation
4. **Memory decay**: Learning then forgetting

### Our Approach

1. **Active detection**: Random questions force you to face weak areas
2. **Instant feedback**: Detailed explanations and improvement suggestions
3. **Interactive learning**: Ask follow-ups, not one-way consumption
4. **Systematic tracking**: Progress by topic and difficulty ensures full coverage

### Key Advantages

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Targeted | Designed specifically for FE interviews, not generic finance |
| Comprehensive | 264 questions, 23 topics, 3 difficulty levels |
| Interactive | Not static content—ask questions, go deeper, personalize |
| Efficient | AI handles question lookup and explanations |
| Realistic | Interview mode simulates real Q&A; learning mode enables deep understanding |
