# Tech Interview Know-How Mastery

## Overview

**The Problem with Traditional Interview Prep:**

You've used Kafka, Spark, MongoDB—it's all on your resume. But when the interviewer asks "Why did you choose Kafka?" your mind goes blank.

It's not that you can't use these technologies. It's that you've never thought about: **Why does it exist? What did people use before? When should you NOT use it?**

These are **Know-How questions**. Interviewers ask almost exclusively Know-How questions. Most candidates prepare almost exclusively Know-What answers.

**Our Solution:**

A systematic AI-assisted learning process that takes you from "I've used this technology" to "I truly understand this technology"—finding your blind spots before interviewers do, then filling them in.

| Traditional Approach | Our Approach |
|---------------------|--------------|
| Accumulate Know-How through years of experience | Systematically uncover blind spots with AI |
| Read docs, memorize concepts | AI-tailored content based on YOUR resume |
| Discover you can't articulate during actual interviews | Mock interviews expose problems early |
| Start from scratch for every interview | Build a personal knowledge base that compounds |

## Module 1: Blind Spot Diagnosis

### The Problem

There's an invisible gap between "I've used Kafka" and "I understand Kafka." You don't see it—until an interviewer asks the right question.

Most people discover their blind spots during actual interviews. By then, it's too late.

### The Solution

AI analyzes your resume and target role, automatically generating Know-How questions tailored to your background—exactly what interviewers will ask.

You simply tag each question honestly:
- **Blank**: Can't answer at all, needs deep study
- **Fuzzy**: Can say something but not clearly, needs clarification
- **Solid**: Can answer confidently, interview strength

This becomes your blind spot map—a precise study roadmap before your interview.

### Personalization

- Questions generated from **your resume**, not generic tech Q&A
- Aligned with **target role requirements**, focusing on most likely topics
- Can drill deeper into specific technologies for more granular questions

## Module 2: Technical Deep Dive

### The Problem

You've found your blind spots. Now how do you fill them?

Search engine results are either too shallow (tell you how to use it, not why) or too scattered (bits and pieces that don't connect). What you need is a technical mentor who understands your background and can explain accordingly.

### The Solution

AI becomes your private technical mentor. You provide the question and your resume, and it:

1. **Asks clarifying questions first**—figures out where exactly you're stuck
2. **Generates a custom overview**—tailored to your background, question, and desired depth
3. **Supports unlimited follow-ups**—each one deepens your understanding

After this, the technology isn't just a "point" on your resume—it's a "line" with context and history.

### Personalization

- AI sees your project experience, knows how to approach the explanation
- Not generic encyclopedia content, but answers to **your specific question**
- Through follow-up questions, you learn to ask sharper questions—that's progress itself

### Output Example

A complete technical overview covering:
- Why does this technology exist? What problem does it solve?
- What did people use before it?
- What's the fundamental difference from alternatives?
- When should you NOT use it?
- Key design decisions and trade-offs

## Module 3: Know-How Mock Interview

### The Problem

You've built knowledge. You've taken notes. But here's the question: **Can you actually articulate it under interview pressure?**

Knowing and speaking are different things. When typing, you have time to organize. When speaking, you don't.

### The Solution

AI plays a real interviewer—not a scripted Q&A, but one that probes, pushes, and forces you to be clear.

Flow:
1. Provide resume + JD + one Know-How question
2. AI asks like a real interviewer, probes up to three rounds
3. You answer with **voice**, in **English**
4. AI gives feedback and checks your knowledge base

Key innovation: AI tells you whether **you studied this but forgot, or never studied it at all**. Forgot? Go review. Never studied? Go back and learn. This closes the loop.

### Personalization

- Follow-up questions based on **what you actually said**, not pre-written scripts
- Feedback identifies **your specific issues**: unclear articulation or knowledge gaps
- Knowledge base check tells you exactly **what to do next**

## Module 4: Project Deep Dive Mock Interview

### The Problem

Know-How tests whether you understand a technology. But interviews have another dimension: **Can you clearly explain what you've done?**

In Project Deep Dive interviews, interviewers pick a resume project and dig deep:
- What was the situation? What problem were you solving?
- What did **you** specifically do (not the team—you)?
- Why did you make those decisions?
- What challenges did you face? How did you solve them?

Most people fail not because they didn't do good work—but because they can't articulate it under pressure.

### The Solution

AI plays interviewer, digging into your project from multiple angles:

- Background and problem definition
- Your personal contribution ("I did," not "we did")
- The "why" behind decisions
- Challenges and solutions
- Quantifiable results and impact
- Reflection and improvement opportunities

After 5-8 rounds of probing, you get structured feedback: narrative clarity, technical explanation ability, self-awareness depth.

### Personalization

- Questions based on **your actual resume projects**
- Follow-ups dynamically adjusted based on **your answers**
- Feedback addresses **your** narrative habits: rambling, skipping the why, or blurring personal vs. team contributions

## Why This Works

### Problems with Traditional Prep

1. **Surface-level learning**: You know how to use it, not why. Knowledge stays isolated. You get stumped when interviewers probe.

2. **Passive experience accumulation**: Waiting to learn Know-How through work. But interviews won't wait for your third year of experience—they ask now.

3. **Discovering problems too late**: First time you realize you can't articulate is during the actual interview. By then, the offer is gone.

4. **Starting from scratch every time**: No systematic accumulation. What you prepped for the last interview? You have to redo it.

### Our Approach

1. **Proactive blind spot discovery**: Find your knowledge gaps before interviewers do.

2. **Personalized learning**: AI generates content based on your resume and questions—not generic tutorials.

3. **Pressure-test articulation**: Practice speaking in a safe environment. Find problems before you lose offers.

4. **Compounding knowledge base**: Every overview, every practice session gets saved. The base grows thicker, prep gets faster.

### Core Advantages

- **Tailored to you**: All questions, overviews, and mocks based on your background
- **Closed-loop system**: Diagnose → Learn → Test → Find new gaps → Keep learning
- **Visible progress**: Blind spot map changes are visible, knowledge base growth is visible
- **Lifetime asset**: Knowledge base follows your career—never starting from zero again
